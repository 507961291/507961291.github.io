Target Audience

Description

Middle aged, professional Idahoans who stay active and informed to assist them in decision making and sound leadership. Although this is a strictly family-friendly site, the audience is adults. Generally, they are leaders in the workplace and in the home. They are breadwinners and are resposible for, not only their families, but their community as a whole, also.

Personas

Forest Conservationist Tim
Tim has been working with the US Forest Service since he was 18. He's only a few years shy of his 20th workiversary. He leads projects in trail development and maintenance, taking into consideration various efforts to protect and revive native plant and wildlife species. Before heading out on the trail, Tim corresponds with his team and receives orders from regional directors from the US Forest Service office. He will likely check the conditions from his desktop workstation.
Scenarios:
  1. Tim needs to create a detour after a recent fire has devastated a highly trafficed route. He visits Idaho Weather Site to see if there has been rain in the area over the last 48 hours or expected in the next week, as he would like to build a temporary bridge for the detour.
  2. Tim will act as docent for some visiting biologists who are curious about the a specific type of endangered falcon. Tim checks Idaho Weather Site to find out the moisture levels of the air. He wants to make sure the fog doesn't interfere with their visibility.
  3. Tim heard that a nearby storm has taken an unexpected turn and will be interfering with a renaturalization project he hoped to have completed before a new group is expected to be camping in the area. He checks Idaho Weather Site to see if he has enough time to complete his project, or if he should call the camp event off.
Equestrian Endurance Rider Rachel
Rachel is in her 40s and owns and runs a barn where local community members or visitors can board their horses. Horse people who wish to explore this region of Idaho can rent horses and a trail guide to explore the wilderness by horseback as a day trip. Weather conditions will dictate the routes taken. Rachel likes to talk and make plans, but isn't one to sit at a desk. She will likely check the site from her mobile phone.
Scenarios:
  1. Rachel has a group of local riders and their support crews getting ready for a week long trip out of state to ride in a 100 Mile Edurance Race together. She checks Idaho Weather Site to determine when the ideal times to go on community bonding trail rides.
  2. Rachel is showing her barn to a family who is new to the area and having their house built. They would like to board their horse whlie their house is being built. Rachel wants it to be a highly accessible time for the family to see her accomodations.
